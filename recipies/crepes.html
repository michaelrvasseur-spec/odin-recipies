Crepes

Homemade crepes are one of those recipes that look impressive but are surprisingly simple once you get the feel for them. The batter comes together in minutes with just a handful of pantry staples, and the only real technique involved is learning to swirl the pan quickly so the batter spreads into that signature paper-thin round. The key differentiator between a good crepe and a great one is resting the batter — giving the flour time to fully absorb the liquid makes the texture noticeably more silky and tender.

What makes crepes so appealing is their versatility. Go sweet with Nutella and banana, lemon juice and powdered sugar, or fresh whipped cream and berries — or skip the sugar and vanilla entirely and go savory with ham and melted Gruyère or spinach and ricotta. Either way, they come together fast once the batter is made, and a batch can easily feed a crowd for breakfast or dessert.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup all purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1.5 cups whole milk
  • 2 tbs unstaled butter, melted
  • 1 tbs sugar
  • 0.25 tsp salt
  • 0.6 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbs butter for the pan

Steps

  1. Make the batter: In a blender or large bowl, combine 1 cups all-purpose flour, 2 pieces eggs, 1.5 cups whole milk, 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted, 1 tablespoons sugar, 0.3 teaspoons salt, and 0.5 teaspoons vanilla extract. Blend or whisk until completely smooth with no lumps.
  2. Rest the batter: Cover the batter and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 24 hours). This lets the flour hydrate fully and results in much more tender crepes — don't skip this step.
  3. Heat the pan: Heat a nonstick skillet or crepe pan over medium heat. Add a small pat of 1 tablespoons butter (for the pan) and swirl to coat the pan. The pan is ready when a drop of water flicked onto it sizzles immediately.
  4. Cook the first crepe: Pour about 3–4 tablespoons of batter into the center of the pan. Immediately lift the pan and swirl it in a circular motion to spread the batter into a thin, even round. Cook for about 60–1m 15s 01:15 until the edges look dry and the bottom is lightly golden.
  5. Flip and finish: Slide a thin spatula under the edge and flip the crepe. Cook for another 20–25 seconds 00:25 on the second side, then slide onto a plate. The second side will be spottier than the first — that's normal.
  6. Repeat: Continue with the remaining batter, adding a small amount of 1 tablespoons butter (for the pan) to the pan every few crepes as needed. Stack finished crepes on top of each other — they won't stick.
  7. Fill and serve: Serve immediately with your choice of fillings. For sweet crepes: Nutella, fresh berries, whipped cream, lemon and sugar, or jam. For savory crepes: ham and Gruyère, spinach and ricotta, or smoked salmon and cream cheese.
